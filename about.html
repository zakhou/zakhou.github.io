"... it is the struggle itself that is most important. We must strive to be more than we are. It does not matter that we will never reach our ultimate goal. The effort yields its own rewards. [...] It is the difference between knowledge and experience." — Lt. Cmdr. Data

I am currently a postdoctoral astrophysicist at the University of Chicago, transitioning to data science. Having been working with data from telescopes and satellites, I have developed skills for data processing, predictive modeling, and data visualization.

At UChicago, I work with the collaboration of South Pole Telescope (SPT), focusing on the cross-validation of data between SPT and other observations. I also work on the numerical simulation engine used across multiple teams within the collaboration, making sure the communications with teams are accurate and effective and the simulation products meet diverse analysis requirements.

Before moving to Chicago, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the University of California, Davis, working with both Planck and SPT collaborations. My job was to do model training for Planck optical system calibration and the predictive modeling of the evolution of the Universe using Planck and SPT data.
